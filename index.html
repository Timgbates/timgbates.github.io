Uniform Acceleration

Radiation of a uniformly accelerated charge is a persistent problem in the physics literature.
There are several points of debate which arise including but not limited to the following.

  • Nonzero power radiated predicted by the Larmor formula, yet zero radiation reaction
  • Equivalence principle paradox (falling charge radiation paradox)
For an introduction and overview to this area, I recommend reading: Fulton & Rohrlich 1961, Boulware 1980, and Pauri & Vallisneri 1999.
For an overview and somewhat of a resolution to the first point, Ginzburg in his textbook gives a clear argument for how to reconcile the two.
There is a similar argument made in Fulton & Rohrlich which is in some sense the standard.
To become aquainted with the equivalence principle paradox, the papers by Pauri & Vallisneri (1990), Ginzburg (1970), and Fulling & Wilson are great sources. They go through the paradox of the 4 cases of falling & supported charges and observers.
For work on the scalar case, Ren & Weinberg is perhaps the best source. Others including Fulling & Wilson (in 2d spacetime), and Higuchi & Matsas, and Kialka et. al. (for Klein Gordon fields) have also written on the scalar case.
